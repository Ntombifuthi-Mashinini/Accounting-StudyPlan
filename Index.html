Notes & Formulas

Essential Formulas

  • Accounting Equation:

    Assets = Liabilities + Equity

    Explanation: This equation represents the fundamental relationship between a company's assets, liabilities, and equity. It ensures that the balance sheet remains balanced.

  • Net Income:

    Net Income = Revenue - Expenses

    Explanation: Net income is the profit a company earns after deducting all expenses from its total revenue.

  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS):

    COGS = Opening Inventory + Purchases - Closing Inventory

    Explanation: COGS represents the direct costs of producing goods sold by a company. It includes the cost of materials and labor directly used in production.

  • Gross Profit:

    Gross Profit = Sales Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold

    Explanation: Gross profit measures the profitability of a company's core business activities before deducting operating expenses.

  • Debt-to-Equity Ratio:

    Debt-to-Equity Ratio = Total Liabilities / Total Equity

    Explanation: This ratio measures a company's financial leverage by comparing its total liabilities to its shareholders' equity.

Paper 1 Specific Formulas

  • Current Ratio:

    Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

    Explanation: This ratio measures a company's ability to pay its short-term obligations with its short-term assets.

  • Acid Test Ratio:

    Acid Test Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities

    Explanation: Also known as the quick ratio, it measures a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations without relying on the sale of inventory.

  • Inventory Turnover Ratio:

    Inventory Turnover Ratio = Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory

    Explanation: This ratio measures how efficiently a company manages its inventory by comparing the cost of goods sold to the average inventory level.

  • Profit Margin:

    Profit Margin = Net Income / Sales Revenue

    Explanation: This ratio measures the percentage of revenue that remains as profit after all expenses are deducted.

Paper 2 Specific Formulas

  • Break-Even Point:

    Break-Even Point = Fixed Costs / (Selling Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit)

    Explanation: This is the point at which total revenue equals total costs, resulting in neither profit nor loss.

  • Contribution Margin:

    Contribution Margin = Selling Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit

    Explanation: This measures the amount of revenue remaining after variable costs are deducted, which contributes to covering fixed costs and generating profit.

  • Margin of Safety:

    Margin of Safety = (Actual Sales - Break-Even Sales) / Actual Sales

    Explanation: This measures the difference between actual sales and break-even sales, indicating how much sales can drop before the company incurs a loss.

  • Budget Variance:

    Budget Variance = Actual Amount - Budgeted Amount

    Explanation: This measures the difference between actual financial performance and the budgeted amount, helping to identify deviations.

Important Points to Remember

  • Understanding the concepts behind the formulas is crucial: beyond just memorizing the equations.
  • Practice applying the formulas to different scenarios: to solidify your understanding.
  • Always double-check your calculations: to avoid errors in your financial analysis.
